# PrivateBox — King V-Aligned Corporate Governance Architecture

## 1. Executive Summary

PrivateBox can credibly position itself as a **scaling private South African technology company whose governance architecture is designed in accordance with the King V Code on Corporate Governance for South Africa, 2025 (King V)**. King V was officially launched by the Institute of Directors in South Africa (IoDSA) and the King Committee on **31 October 2025**, and is effective for financial years commencing on or after **1 January 2026**, with early adoption strongly encouraged. King V supersedes King IV in its entirety. It comprises four integrally connected documents: the **King V Code**, **King V Foundational Concepts**, **King V Glossary**, and the **King V Disclosure Framework** (the latter being a *required* element for any organisation that wishes to *claim application of King V*).

King V is **a voluntary, principles-based, outcomes-focused governance code — not a certifiable management-system standard**. It cannot be "certified", and there is no such thing as "King V compliance" in the ISO sense. Organisations apply the principles, explain non-adoption or modification of recommended practices, and provide a concluding statement on whether the four governance outcomes have been realised: **Ethical Culture; Performance and Value Creation; Conformance and Prudent Control; Legitimacy**. PrivateBox should therefore use language such as "**aligned with King V principles**", "**governance architecture structured in accordance with King V**", "**evidence-based alignment with King V governance expectations**", and "**apply-and-explain governance approach informed by King V**".

For PrivateBox specifically — an on-prem, single-tenant, founder-led AI vendor serving high-trust enterprise clients in regulated sectors — King V is unusually well-suited as a **unifying governance overlay** above its parallel work on ISO 27001, ISO 27701, POPIA, NIST AI RMF, NIST SSDF/SP 800-218A and the EU AI Act. King V's **Principle 10 (data, information and technology)** explicitly addresses AI human oversight, accountability, transparency, explainability, security, privacy, fairness and trustworthiness, plus emerging-technology risk and supplier governance. **Principle 8 (risk)**, **Principle 9 (compliance)**, **Principle 12 (assurance)** and **Principle 13 (stakeholders)** allow PrivateBox to consolidate cybersecurity, privacy, AI, supplier, and customer-trust governance into a single board-evidenced narrative. **Proportionality** is built into King V — a scaling private tech company is expected to *scale practices* rather than copy a JSE-listed multinational, but is *not* permitted to dilute the principles themselves.

This output translates that mandate into a granular control library, evidence pack, board architecture, disclosure logic, crosswalk to other frameworks, market-practice scan, and roadmap. The single most important early action is for PrivateBox's governing body to **adopt a King V-aligned board charter, formally appoint at least one independent non-executive director, establish a single combined Audit/Risk/Technology committee charter, and approve a King V Disclosure Framework draft** — signalling early adoption while remaining proportionate.

## 2. Assumptions and Scoping Notes for PrivateBox

- **Legal form**: PrivateBox is assumed to be a South African private company (Pty) Ltd, founder-led, scaling, not JSE-listed, but selling into JSE-listed and regulated enterprise clients (banks, insurers, law firms, healthcare, professional services).
- **Public Interest Score (PI Score)**: As PrivateBox grows, its PI Score will likely cross thresholds requiring statutory audit and a statutory **social and ethics committee** under the Companies Act 71 of 2008 (Regulation 43). The architecture below is designed so this transition is incremental, not disruptive.
- **Product profile**: On-prem, single-tenant AI deployed inside the client environment; sensitive data does not leave the client. Optional support layers (remote admin, telemetry, update delivery, monitoring, backup, vendor maintenance, support access, managed connectors) require explicit governance treatment — they are the *only* points where PrivateBox or its sub-processors touch client data or client systems and so are the focal point of supplier and AI-supply-chain disclosures.
- **Regulatory overlay**: POPIA (binding); JSE Listings Requirements (only relevant to enterprise clients, not PrivateBox itself, but creates a disclosure expectation downstream); ISO 27001/27701; NIST AI RMF 1.0 (and Generative AI Profile); NIST SP 800-218A (SSDF); EU AI Act (relevant where PrivateBox is *deployed* into the EU or for EU-affecting outputs); the (currently in flux) South African National AI Policy Framework, which itself references King-style human oversight obligations.
- **Voluntary application**: PrivateBox voluntarily elects to *apply and explain* King V from FY commencing on or after 1 January 2026, with early-adoption signalling in trust pack from now.
- **Proportionality lens**: Principles apply universally; recommended practices are scaled to PrivateBox's size, business-model complexity, ownership structure, and economic/social/environmental impact, with **compensating measures** documented where practices are not adopted in their full enterprise form.
- **No certification claim**: Throughout, PrivateBox avoids "King V certified", "fully King V compliant", or "guaranteed King V compliance".

## 5. Table 2 — King V Principle-by-Principle Matrix

For brevity, columns are condensed but each principle is treated at granular level. Columns: **#** | **Principle (authoritative summary)** | **Plain-English** | **PrivateBox relevance** | **Applies?** | **Owner** | **Control objective + checklist** | **Artifacts** | **Inspectable evidence** | **Test/validate** | **Maturity indicator** | **Pitfalls** | **Disclosure expectation** | **External-safe wording** | **Source**

**P1 — Leadership.** *"The governing body leads ethically and effectively as the focal point of corporate governance in the organisation."* Plain: the board sets and embodies tone, integrity, competence, responsibility, accountability, fairness and transparency. **PrivateBox**: founder-led culture must be balanced with collective board accountability. **Applies**: Yes. **Owner**: Board (collective). **Checklist**: ☐ Approve board charter (Practice 4); ☐ each director cultivates the six characteristics (Practice 1); ☐ written declaration of all financial / professional / business interests at least annually + at each meeting (Practices 39–40); ☐ formal performance evaluation of board, committees, chair and individuals at least every 2 years (Practices 6–9); ☐ integrity, competence, responsibility, accountability, fairness, transparency embedded in evaluation criteria. **Artifacts**: Board charter; signed director letters of appointment; conflicts register; board evaluation report; board minutes. **Inspectable evidence**: charter PDF; latest evaluation summary; conflicts register sample (redacted). **Test**: ask a director "describe how the charter was last reviewed" and inspect the minute. **Maturity**: charter reviewed annually; biennial external evaluation. **Pitfalls**: founder/CEO acting as both Chair and CEO (Practice 48 prohibits) without lead independent; charter copied from a template not lived. **Disclosure**: Principle 1 narrative + concluding statement. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox's governing body operates under a board charter and director conduct expectations aligned with King V Principle 1." **Source**: King V Code Principle 1, Practices 1–9.

**P2 — Ethics.** *"The governing body governs the ethics of the organisation in a way that enables an ethical culture and responsible corporate citizenship."* **Plain**: ethics is governed, not assumed. **PrivateBox**: critical because clients trust PrivateBox not to misuse data. **Owner**: Board, may delegate to social and ethics committee (statutory if PI Score triggers Reg 43). **Checklist**: ☐ board approves ethics policy / code of conduct (Practice 12); ☐ AI ethics statement (linked to Principle 10's AI values); ☐ anti-corruption, anti-fraud, anti-money-laundering programmes (Practice 12c); ☐ protected whistleblowing channel (Practice 13d); ☐ ethics built into hiring, performance, supplier onboarding (Practice 13b); ☐ workplace, economy, society, environment measures and targets (Practice 18). **Artifacts**: code of ethics; AI ethics statement; whistleblowing policy + channel; anti-corruption policy; corporate-citizenship report; supplier code. **Inspectable evidence**: training records; whistleblowing statistics; ethics committee minutes; sanctions log. **Test**: trace a reported ethics matter from intake to closure. **Maturity**: independent whistleblower line; periodic assurance on ethics effectiveness (Practice 14). **Pitfalls**: ethics confused with compliance; whistleblower channel that routes to the implicated party. **Disclosure**: how ethics is governed and what indicators of ethical culture exist. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox's ethics framework, anti-corruption controls and protected whistleblowing process are governed by the board in accordance with King V Principle 2." **Source**: King V Practices 10–18.

**P3 — Strategy, performance and sustainable value creation.** *"The governing body ensures that the organisation's purpose, strategy and business model support performance that creates sustainable value within the organisation's economic, social and environmental context."* **Plain**: purpose → strategy → business model → measurable value. **PrivateBox**: must articulate why on-prem confidential AI is durable value, including systems-value (data sovereignty, sector resilience). **Owner**: Board (steers and approves), management (executes). **Checklist**: ☐ approved purpose statement; ☐ board-approved strategy and operational plan with KPIs (Practices 19–22); ☐ short/medium/long-term performance targets; ☐ integrated-thinking review of the six capitals; ☐ negative-impact mitigation (e.g., AI-misuse risk, energy footprint, employment effects); ☐ ongoing oversight of going-concern, solvency, liquidity. **Artifacts**: strategy doc; budget; KPI scorecard; capital impact narrative; going-concern memo. **Inspectable evidence**: strategy minute; quarterly strategy review; CFO solvency/liquidity reporting. **Maturity**: integrated thinking explicitly applied; board-level strategy debate documented. **Pitfalls**: strategy = sales targets; no consideration of negative impacts. **Disclosure**: purpose, strategy, KPIs, value creation narrative. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox's strategy is approved and overseen by the governing body using integrated thinking aligned with King V Principle 3." **Source**: Practices 19–22.

**P4 — Reporting.** *"The governing body ensures that external reports … enable stakeholders to make informed assessments of how the organisation creates, preserves and erodes value."* **Plain**: external reports must enable holistic stakeholder assessment, including double materiality. **PrivateBox**: even as a private company, the trust pack, security disclosures, AI factsheets, and governance section function as external reports. **Owner**: Board (approves) — may delegate integrity oversight to audit committee. **Checklist**: ☐ board approves the suite of external reports (Practice 25b); ☐ annual integrated report (or proportionate equivalent — for a private company, an *integrated trust and governance pack*); ☐ King V Disclosure Framework published on the website (Practice 27c); ☐ double-materiality applied (financial + impact) (Practice 26); ☐ integrity controls over reports. **Artifacts**: integrated trust pack; King V Disclosure Framework PDF; AFS; AI factsheet; cybersecurity disclosure; governance section. **Inspectable evidence**: board minute approving each report; assurance statements; published links. **Maturity**: integrated narrative ties strategy, risk, AI, security, privacy into one coherent stakeholder assessment. **Pitfalls**: marketing copy mistaken for governance disclosure; King V Disclosure Framework absent. **Disclosure**: the Disclosure Framework itself + concluding statement on outcomes. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox publishes a governance disclosure structured in accordance with the King V Disclosure Framework." **Source**: Practices 23–27; Disclosure Framework.

**P5 — Composition of the governing body.** *"The governing body ensures that its composition is balanced with respect to the mix of competencies, diversity and independence …"* **Plain**: balanced board, majority non-executive, most independent, diverse, independence assessed holistically. **PrivateBox**: founder-led — must consciously over-correct for independence. **Owner**: Board (and nominations committee or equivalent). **Checklist (proportionate)**: ☐ majority non-executive members, *most* of whom independent (Practice 29) — for PrivateBox, target at least one independent NED at minimum, scaling to majority pre-IPO; ☐ CEO + at least one other executive on the board (Practice 30); ☐ diversity across competencies, age, culture, race, gender (Practice 31); ☐ staggered rotation (Practice 32); ☐ succession plan for board, chair, lead independent, committee chairs (Practice 33); ☐ skills matrix; ☐ annual interest declarations + per-meeting declarations (Practices 39–40); ☐ independence holistically assessed against the nine factors in Practice 42 (significant funder, securities-significance, executive within 3 yrs, audit-team within 3 yrs, significant supplier/customer, related-party, **>9 yrs tenure**, performance-contingent remuneration); ☐ background checks and verified qualifications for nominees (Practice 37c). **Artifacts**: skills matrix; director CVs; appointment letters; conflicts register; nominations committee terms of reference; succession plan. **Maturity**: independence rationale documented for any director near the line; nine-year tenure reviewed annually. **Pitfalls**: founder-controlled board with no independents; cosmetic NED who is in fact a customer/supplier; "independence by assertion". **Disclosure**: full director profiles; tenure; categorisation; rationale for independence calls. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox's board composition is structured to deliver the balance of competencies, diversity and independence contemplated by King V Principle 5, applied proportionately to a scaling private technology company." **Source**: Practices 28–44.

**P6 — Committees of the governing body.** *"…arrangements for delegation to committees and individuals … promote the objective and effective discharge of its obligations."* **Plain**: committees with written terms of reference, ≥3 members, balanced. **PrivateBox proportionate model**: ☐ Combined **Audit, Risk and Technology** Committee (one TOR, 3 members, majority non-executive, at least one independent — under King V both risk and S&E must include at least one independent NED); ☐ Combined **Social, Ethics and Remuneration** Committee (also incorporates corporate citizenship and remuneration governance); ☐ optional **AI Oversight Sub-committee** or AI working group reporting in (proportionate substitute for a dedicated AI committee). **Statutory note**: once the PI Score crosses thresholds, the social and ethics committee under Companies Act s.72(4) and Reg 43 becomes mandatory; PrivateBox should pre-build the architecture. **Practices**: 51–56 (general), 57–62 (audit), 63–65 (nominations), 66–68 (risk — at least one independent NED), 69–72 (remuneration — all NED, majority independent, independent NED chair), 73–75 (social and ethics — at least one independent NED). **Artifacts**: each committee's TOR; minutes; chair reports to board. **Pitfalls**: chair of board chairing the audit committee (Practice 49a prohibits); CEO sitting on audit/remuneration/nominations as a member (Practice 78 prohibits, but may attend by invitation). **External-safe**: "PrivateBox operates a proportionate committee architecture aligned to King V Principle 6, including a combined audit, risk and technology committee and a social, ethics and remuneration committee."

**P7 — Appointment and delegation to management.** *"…appointment and delegation to management promote operational effectiveness and that the respective roles and decision-making powers of the governing body and management are clearly defined."* **Plain**: clear CEO role; delegation of authority framework; CEO succession; access to professional corporate governance services (company secretary or equivalent). **PrivateBox**: founder/CEO must not also chair (Practice 48); a 3-year cooling off applies before retired CEO can chair. **Checklist**: ☐ CEO formally appointed and reports to the board (Practice 76); ☐ CEO not a member of audit/remuneration/nominations (Practice 78); ☐ board-approved delegation of authority framework (Practice 82); ☐ CEO succession plan (Practice 79); ☐ CEO performance led by chair (Practice 80); ☐ company secretary or equivalent provides independent corporate-governance services (Practices 84–90). **Pitfall**: founder retains undocumented decision-making power; no cosec equivalent. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox operates a board-approved delegation-of-authority framework and CEO succession discipline aligned with King V Principle 7." **Source**: Practices 76–90.

**P8 — Risk.** *"The governing body governs risk in a way that enables the organisation to sustain and optimise its strategy and objectives."* **Plain**: risk is opportunity-aware, integrated, iterative, and resilient. **PrivateBox**: cyber, AI model, supplier, business-continuity, key-person, regulatory, and reputational risks are the dominant cluster. **Checklist**: ☐ approved risk policy and frameworks (Practice 92); ☐ documented risk appetite and tolerance (Practice 93b–c); ☐ risk register covering economic/social/environmental context (Practice 93a); ☐ business continuity and resilience arrangements with testing (Practice 93d); ☐ risk integrated into day-to-day operations (Practice 93e); ☐ iterative, change-responsive (Practice 93f); ☐ periodic assurance on risk function and ICF (Practice 94). **PrivateBox-specific**: model drift, prompt-injection, supply-chain compromise of foundation models / model weights / containers, telemetry channel abuse, on-prem update channel integrity, key-person dependency on founder. **External-safe**: "Risk governance at PrivateBox is structured to deliver the outcomes contemplated by King V Principle 8, with explicit treatment of cyber, AI model, supplier, and business-continuity risks." **Source**: Practices 91–94.

**P9 — Compliance.** *"The governing body governs compliance with applicable laws and adopted policies, non-binding rules, codes and standards in a way that promotes ethics and responsible corporate citizenship."* **Plain**: compliance is ethics-aware and rights-aware, not just rule-following. **Checklist**: ☐ board-approved compliance framework (Practice 97); ☐ obligations register (POPIA; Cybercrimes Act; ECTA; Companies Act; sector regulator obligations of clients that pass through to PrivateBox; EU AI Act extra-territorial reach); ☐ adopted non-binding standards explicitly listed (ISO 27001, ISO 27701, NIST AI RMF, NIST SSDF, EU AI Act voluntary code of practice for GPAI providers, King V itself); ☐ regulatory horizon scanning (Practice 98d); ☐ periodic assurance on compliance function (Practice 99). **External-safe**: "PrivateBox's compliance framework is governed at board level and addresses both binding obligations and adopted non-binding standards in alignment with King V Principle 9."

**P10 — Data, information and technology (the King V flagship update).** *"The governing body governs data, information and technology in a way that enables the organisation to sustain and optimise its strategy and objectives."* This is the highest-leverage principle for PrivateBox. King V splits this into a **Data and Information** branch (Practices 100–104) and a **Technology** branch (Practices 105–110). **Data and information checklist**: ☐ board approves data and information policies (Practice 102); ☐ lifecycle controls — acquisition, creation, use, dissemination, disposal (Practice 103a); ☐ ethical/responsible management (103b); ☐ legal compliance (103c); ☐ classification of sensitive data (103d); ☐ confidentiality, integrity, availability (103e); ☐ privacy of personal data (103f); ☐ data quality (103g); ☐ third-party / cross-border data risk management (103h); ☐ periodic assurance (Practice 104). **Technology checklist**: ☐ board approves technology policies (Practice 107); ☐ legal compliance (108a); ☐ resilience, disaster recovery, testing (108b); ☐ benefit realisation vs investment (108c); ☐ responsible disposal (108d); ☐ effective cybersecurity (108e); ☐ outsourced/third-party tech assurance with minimum control assurance (108f). **Emerging tech / AI checklist (Practice 109c)**: ☐ AI systems adhere to **ethics, human centricity, accountability, transparency, explainability, security, privacy, fairness, trustworthiness**; ☐ clear accountability for AI decisions, actions, outputs and outcomes; ☐ **human oversight and override mechanisms commensurate with risk**. **Periodic assurance** on technology effectiveness, compliance and ethics (Practice 110). **PrivateBox interpretation**: this is where on-prem architecture earns its keep — the policy must explicitly state what telemetry, update, support and connector channels exist, what data they touch, what consent and control the client has, and how human override works for AI outputs. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox governs data, information, technology and AI through board-approved policies designed to deliver the human-oversight, accountability, transparency, explainability, security, privacy, fairness and trustworthiness expectations of King V Principle 10." **Source**: Practices 100–110.

**P11 — Remuneration.** *"…the organisation remunerates fairly, responsibly and transparently to promote sustainable value creation …"* King V is now less prescriptive, deferring to s.30(4) Companies Act and the Companies Amendment Act 16 of 2024 (not yet in force). **PrivateBox checklist**: ☐ board-approved remuneration policy (Practice 113); ☐ fair-and-responsible test between executive and overall employee remuneration (Practice 114b); ☐ malus/clawback for variable pay (Practice 114e); ☐ NED fee policy submitted to shareholders if applicable (Practice 115); ☐ shareholder engagement on remuneration (Practice 116); ☐ for entities required to have a S&E committee, non-binding advisory votes on policy (every 3 years or material change) and on the implementation report (annual) (Practice 118). **Pitfall**: founder-set salaries with no committee discipline; absence of malus/clawback for AI-misuse-linked variable pay. **External-safe**: "PrivateBox operates a fair, responsible and transparent remuneration framework aligned to King V Principle 11."

**P12 — Assurance.** *"…assurance functions and services promote an effective internal control environment and safeguard the integrity of external reports."* **Plain**: combined assurance across line management, specialist functions, internal audit, external audit, specialist providers, regulators. **PrivateBox proportionate model**: ☐ combined assurance map covering ISO 27001/27701 surveillance, NIST AI RMF self-assessment, SOC 2 Type II (if pursued), penetration testing, internal financial controls review, statutory audit (when triggered), and product-level red-team / model-evaluation reports; ☐ for each external report (trust pack, governance disclosure, AI factsheet) state level of assurance and criteria (Practice 126); ☐ internal audit charter and risk-based plan (Practices 129–130) — *may be co-sourced for a scaling firm* (Practice 131c); ☐ external independent review of internal audit at least once every 5 years (Practice 131f). **External-safe**: "PrivateBox operates a combined assurance model designed to support effective internal controls and the integrity of external reports in alignment with King V Principle 12."

**P13 — Stakeholders.** *"The governing body adopts a stakeholder-inclusive approach in the execution of its duties in the long-term best interests of the organisation within its economic, social and environmental context."* **Plain**: identify, weigh, balance stakeholder interests; uphold Ubuntu-Botho. **PrivateBox stakeholder map**: clients (especially regulated-sector procurement and CISO/DPO functions); client end-users (data subjects); regulators (Information Regulator, sector regulators); sub-processors and model/foundation-model providers; employees; founders/investors; community/society. **Checklist**: ☐ board-approved stakeholder engagement framework (Practice 134); ☐ stakeholder methodology (Practice 135a); ☐ risk/opportunity analysis from stakeholder interests (135b); ☐ dispute resolution (135c); ☐ periodic assurance on stakeholder management (136); ☐ **shareholder engagement** practices (Practices 137–144) — for PrivateBox, this scales to investor relations and material customer relationship management; ☐ **group governance framework** if PrivateBox has subsidiaries (Practices 145–150). **External-safe**: "PrivateBox applies a stakeholder-inclusive approach to governance, designed to support the King V outcomes of legitimacy and sustainable value creation." **Source**: Practices 132–150.

## 6. Table 3 — Recommended Practices / Implementation Matrix (selected high-impact practices)

- **P1-P4 (board charter)**: charter approved and periodically reviewed. Checklist: roles, responsibilities, membership, conduct, meeting procedures, reserved matters, delegation. Artifact: signed Board Charter v1.0. Owner: Board / Cosec. Cadence: annual review. Pitfall: template charter with no PrivateBox-specific reserved matters (e.g., approval of new sub-processor, approval of foundation-model change, approval of telemetry policy change).

## 9. Table 6 — Disclosure Framework / Disclosure Matrix

The King V Disclosure Framework is **mandatory for any organisation claiming application of King V**, must be **approved by the governing body**, must be **published on the organisation's website**, and must include a **concluding statement on the four governance outcomes**. PrivateBox should publish a "King V Governance Disclosure" page each year.

For each of P1–P13, the structure is: (a) narrative on application of the principle, (b) declaration of any recommended practices not adopted with explanation and compensating measures, (c) links to other reports where relevant, (d) the closing concluding statement at the end of the document.

**Selected disclosure rows** (theme | King V expectation | what to disclose | required evidence | good language | weak/risky language | tick-box failure | external-safe wording for PrivateBox | source):

- **Concluding statement on outcomes**: required by Disclosure Framework. Disclose: in the board's opinion whether application of principles and practices has realised value per Ethical Culture / Performance and Value Creation / Conformance and Prudent Control / Legitimacy. Good: "The board has applied its mind to the principles of King V and is of the opinion that, having regard to the proportionate practices implemented and the compensating measures described in this document, the four governance outcomes have been substantially realised within PrivateBox's economic, social and environmental context for the financial year." Weak: "PrivateBox is fully King V compliant." (Avoid.) Tick-box failure: copy-paste statement with no evidence trail.
- **Leadership (P1)**: disclose how directors are held to account for ethical and effective leadership. Evidence: charter; evaluation. Good: "The board is governed by a board charter approved on [date], evaluates its performance every two years, and has implemented a conflicts and characteristics framework." Weak: "Our directors lead ethically." Risky: implying the founder embodies governance.
- **Composition (P5)**: disclose number of directors, NEDs, independents, tenure, qualifications, gender, race, age. Evidence: skills matrix; independence assessment. Good: "Of [n] directors, [n] are non-executive, of whom [n] are categorised as independent following holistic assessment against the factors in King V Practice 42." Weak: "Our board is independent." Risky: declaring independence without reasoning.
- **Committees (P6)**: disclose role, composition, qualifications, meetings, attendance, key focus, satisfaction with discharge of TOR. Tick-box failure: omitting attendance.
- **Risk (P8)**: disclose nature and extent of risks willing to take; key risks; significant changes; major incidents. Evidence: risk register summary. Risky language: "We have no material risks."
- **Data, information and technology (P10)**: disclose how data, information and technology are governed; cyber and privacy posture; **AI governance** including human oversight, accountability, transparency, explainability; arrangements for emerging tech. Evidence: AI governance policy; System Cards. Good: "The board oversees AI governance through a board-approved AI policy that operationalises the King V Practice 109c values, requires human oversight commensurate with risk, and is supported by AI System Cards for all production models." Weak: "We use AI responsibly."
- **Remuneration (P11)**: disclose policy summary; fair-and-responsible test; malus/clawback; non-binding advisory votes if applicable.
- **Assurance (P12)**: disclose combined assurance arrangements; level of assurance for each external report.
- **Stakeholders (P13)**: disclose stakeholder methodology; significant interests; how relationships were managed.

**External-safe wording for PrivateBox website / trust pack / procurement responses**:
- Website: "PrivateBox publishes an annual King V Governance Disclosure structured in accordance with the King V Disclosure Framework issued by the Institute of Directors in South Africa."
- Security pack: "PrivateBox's governance, risk, technology, AI, privacy and assurance practices are designed to support the four governance outcomes emphasised by King V (Ethical Culture; Performance and Value Creation; Conformance and Prudent Control; Legitimacy)."
- Procurement RFP: "PrivateBox applies and explains the principles of King V on a proportionate basis appropriate to a scaling private South African technology company. Our governance disclosure is published on our website and updated annually."

**Avoid**: "King V certified", "fully compliant with King V", "guaranteed governance compliance", "King V audited".

## 12. Case-Study / Market-Practice Analysis

At this date (May 2026), almost no full King V Disclosure Framework reports have yet been published — first reporting cycles fall in 2027 for FY2026. The pool below is therefore mixed and clearly labelled.

1. **JSE Limited (Group)** — KING IV, transitioning. Sector: financial market infrastructure. Visible artifacts: principle-by-principle King IV register on group.jse.co.za; explicit board charter, board policies, committee TORs; Group Audit + Group Risk Management Committees; investment in cybersecurity / information security oversight at board. **Lessons for PrivateBox**: principle-by-principle web page is highly readable and procurement-friendly; PrivateBox can mimic structure but at proportionate length. **Don't copy**: enterprise volume.
2. **Sasol Limited** — KING IV, transitioning. Sector: chemicals/energy. Visible: full King IV application disclosure; admits ongoing enhancement of certain practices — model for *honest* "apply and explain". **Lesson**: the disclosure earns credibility precisely *because* it admits work-in-progress.
3. **Standard Bank Group** — KING IV, transitioning. Visible: Governance Report 2024; technology and information committee at board; structured committee architecture; integrated report. **Lesson for PrivateBox**: clear *technology committee* signal at board level — relevant precedent for PrivateBox's combined Audit/Risk/Technology committee.
4. **Discovery Limited** — KING IV. Visible: Governance Report PDFs; Audit, Risk and Compliance, Actuarial, S&E, Nominations, Treating Customers Fairly subcommittee. **Lesson**: customer-focused subcommittee shows how PrivateBox could surface client-trust governance. **Don't copy**: full insurer architecture.
5. **Capitec Bank Holdings** — KING IV. Visible: integrated annual report; conflicts-of-interest policy; clear King IV principle-by-principle commitment; founder-led origin. **Lesson**: founder-origin company that successfully institutionalised — instructive for PrivateBox's founder-to-board transition.
6. **Sibanye-Stillwater** — KING IV. Visible: 2019 King IV application register: states full adoption in plain narrative; clear committee architecture. **Lesson**: simple narrative format works.
7. **TFG (The Foschini Group)** — KING IV. Visible: 2022 King IV register; explicit IT charter; technology and information governance via Audit + Risk Committees with quarterly IT review. **Lesson**: a *combined* approach to tech and risk works at scale and is highly applicable proportionately.
8. **EOH Holdings** — KING IV (technology sector). Visible: 2023 Corporate Governance and Application of King IV Principles; IT Steering Committee; Information and Technology Committee; explicit principle-by-principle statements. **Lesson**: tech company with explicit IT governance committee. **Don't copy**: post-scandal sensitivity — PrivateBox can do this proactively.
9. **Datatec** — KING IV (international ICT). Visible: dedicated *Datatec King IV Application of Principles* document, June 2025 update. **Lesson**: regularly refreshed governance disclosure on a cadence suits a tech firm.
10. **4Sight Holdings** — Explicitly references **the evolving King V framework**. Sector: AI / data / business systems. Visible: March 2026 SENS announcement appointing two independent NEDs and resigning a long-serving NED specifically citing King V and emphasising AI strategy. **Lesson**: a small-to-mid AI-focused JSE-listed firm explicitly using King V language for board-independence rebalancing — *closest analogue for PrivateBox*. **Don't copy**: full JSE-listed disclosure obligations.
11. **NEPI Rockcastle** — KING IV. Visible: detailed Corporate Governance Framework with six pillars (Board and Committees; Group Governance; Management mandate; Compliance and Ethics; Risk Management; Internal Audit). **Lesson**: a six-pillar governance framework is a very portable model PrivateBox can adapt in proportion.
12. **Sabvest Capital (investment holding)** — KING IV. Visible: principle-by-principle disclosures; explicit independence rationale for >9-yr NEDs (now under King V Practice 42h). **Lesson**: how to defend independence near the line.
13. **Pan African Resources** — KING IV. Visible: governance outcome-anchored disclosure (Performance/Effective control etc.); S&E committee. **Lesson**: organising disclosure by outcome is investor-friendly.
14. **Hudaco** — KING IV (industrial). Visible: King IV Application Register integrated into the Integrated Report. **Lesson**: keep it short; embed disclosure in one report.
15. **Karooooo (Cartrack parent, NASDAQ + JSE)** — KING IV + dual-listed. Visible: separately published audit committee charter; compensation and nomination committee charter; code of ethics; anti-bribery and corruption policy; whistleblowing policy; conflict minerals policy; human rights policy. **Lesson**: founder-led tech success story with very clean *individual policy publication* approach. **Highly portable model for PrivateBox** — publish each policy individually on the website.

## 16. Red-Flag Claim List (Never Use)

- ❌ "King V certified"; "King V certification"; "King V audited"; "King V accredited".
- ❌ "Fully King V compliant"; "100% King V compliant"; "guaranteed King V compliance".
- ❌ "ISO-style certification of King V".
- ❌ Any statement implying King V is a binding statute or that PrivateBox is required by law to apply King V (it is voluntary, though courts treat King principles as part of the standard of care).
- ❌ Claims that the on-prem architecture "exempts" PrivateBox from POPIA — POPIA still applies to PrivateBox in its processor and operator capacity.
- ❌ "Our AI is fully ethical" / "fully unbiased" / "fully explainable" — replace with the King V Practice 109c values framing and System Card evidence.
- ❌ "We have no risks." / "No conflicts of interest." (impossible to evidence; instead disclose the framework).
- ❌ "Independent because we say so." Independence must be reasoned holistically against the King V Practice 42 factors.
- ❌ "Founder-led, so governance is simpler." Founder-led firms attract *more* independence and conflict scrutiny under King V.
- ❌ Equating ISO 27001 certification or SOC 2 with King V application — they are complementary but not substitutable.
- ❌ Citing King IV principles or four "King IV outcomes" (Good performance / Effective control). The current outcomes are Performance and Value Creation; Conformance and Prudent Control.

## 17. Final Unified King V Governance Architecture for PrivateBox

PrivateBox's King V-aligned governance architecture rests on **four integrated layers**:

**Layer 1 — Outcomes & Tone:** Board explicitly commits to the four King V outcomes (Ethical Culture; Performance and Value Creation; Conformance and Prudent Control; Legitimacy). The board's annual concluding statement is the apex of the disclosure stack.

**Layer 2 — Board & Committee Architecture (proportionate):**
- Board: founder/CEO + executive(s) + ≥1 Independent NED (target majority NED, most independent within 24 months); independent Chair preferred, otherwise Lead Independent designated.
- Combined **Audit, Risk and Technology Committee** (carries P6/P8/P10/P12 — including AI oversight) — at least one independent NED, chaired by independent NED who is not Chair of board.
- Combined **Social, Ethics and Remuneration Committee** (carries P2/P11/P13) — pre-built to convert to statutory S&E committee on PI Score trigger.
- Management-level **AI Oversight Working Group** reporting in.
- Cosec-as-a-service for independent governance support (P7).

**Layer 3 — Policy and Control Stack (linked across frameworks):**
- Strategic policies: Board Charter; Strategy; DoA; Code of Ethics; Anti-Corruption; Whistleblowing.
- Risk and compliance: ERM Policy; Risk Appetite; Risk Register; Compliance Framework; Obligations Register.
- Technology / Information / Privacy / AI: Information & Technology Governance Policy; ISMS (ISO 27001); PIMS (ISO 27701); POPIA Programme; AI Governance Policy; AI System Cards; Human Oversight SOP; SSDF (NIST 800-218A) practice for the on-prem product build; EU AI Act readiness pack.
- Assurance: Combined Assurance Map; Internal Audit Charter (co-sourced).
- Stakeholders: Stakeholder Engagement Framework; AI Transparency Disclosures to clients.

**Layer 4 — Disclosure and Trust:**
- Annual **King V Governance Disclosure** structured in accordance with the King V Disclosure Framework, approved by the board, published on the website, including the concluding statement on the four outcomes.
- Annual **Trust & Governance Pack** (combining King V disclosure + ISO/NIST/POPIA/EU AI Act posture summary + AI System Cards summary), released under NDA for procurement and investor diligence.
- Standing **King V messaging discipline** across website, security pack, RFP responses, board narrative, and investor decks, using only the safe wording above.

This architecture is deliberately **proportionate**: it captures every King V principle, scales the recommended practices to PrivateBox's stage and complexity, documents compensating measures where practices are not adopted in full enterprise form, leverages on-prem architecture as a positive systems-value proposition (not as an excuse to skip POPIA or AI governance), and unifies the company's parallel work on ISO 27001, ISO 27701, POPIA, NIST AI RMF, NIST SSDF, and the EU AI Act under a single board-evidenced governance narrative.

The result is a defensible, evidence-based alignment with King V governance expectations that will withstand board adviser scrutiny, enterprise procurement diligence, and investor governance review — without overstating PrivateBox's formal status under what is, and remains, a voluntary South African governance code.
